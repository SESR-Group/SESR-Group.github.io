Haotian Ma is a doctoral researcher at the University of Warwick, specializing in applying data-driven methods to accelerate the transition to sustainable energy. With a strong academic background, he has completed programs at the Institute of Data, Systems, and Society at MIT and holds a master's degree from the University of Nottingham. He has also undertaken impactful projects at the University of Birmingham and the Global Energy Interconnection Research Institute.

Haotian obtained his bachelor's degree in engineering from Southeast University, China, where he received numerous honors and was recognized as one of the outstanding graduates of 2019. Throughout his academic career, he has established strong relationships with several prominent industry companies, including the Global Energy Interconnection Research Institute, Huawei European Research Institute, and Nvidia, serving in roles that focused on providing technical support in modeling and coding for AI and energy systems.

With over two years of experience in the energy sector, Haotian has applied data-driven methods to optimize energy systems, reduce carbon emissions, and enhance efficiency and security, contributing to publications and patents in the field. His skills span a range of languages, frameworks, and tools, making him a versatile researcher and engineer in the field of sustainable energy.
